My most recent vacay...

Playa del Carmen, Cancún

I went to Cancún this summer before my sophomore year. My family and I took a flight to Phoenix. From there, we took a second flight to Cancún. The moment we stepped outside, I realized that this was nothing like California. The weather was sweltering and humid, unlike California's hot n dry weather. We rented our car for the trip, so after we picked it up, we drove two hours to get to the resort we were staying in, the Royal Haciendas. I was knocked out for most of the ride, but once we got to the resort, it felt like paradise. We got our keys and went up to the room. I was flabbergasted at the view from our balcony. We had the view of the pools and the ocean right in front of us. We had planned for the trip to be mostly relaxing, with a couple of adventures. Check out my daily entries for more!

Australia

I went to Australia in December to visit my family and my baby cousin. I spent three weeks visiting different cities with my familes. I visited Melbourne, Sydney, Cairns, Adelaide, and Hobart. I fed kangaroos, went on a motor boat trip, saw a sunny Christmas, went underwater motorbiking in the Great Barrier Reef, and so much more. Check out my day-by-day entries for more!

  • June 29

    Birthday Celebrations!

    Happy Birthday to my sister! Today my sister turned 17!! We all got up early and went to breakfast. We took a course on kayaking and then kayaked around on the beach for a while. We walked down the beach until we reached a small hut, where we used their gear and went parasailing. The view was to die for! We went on a scooter further from the coast and sat on a motorboat until it was our turn to go up. After a while, they brought us back down from the air and we went back to land. Not gonna lie, I was pretty happy to be standing on solid things again. My mom, sister, and I went to the spa. After that, we all got ready in nice clothes and took photos to celebrate! We went to the restaurant, where the guitarist played "happy birthday" to my sister, to which she was mortified. It was a great day! :)

  • June 27

    Ziplining and River Rafting

    On my fourth day in Cancún, my family and I decided to go xPlor Park. This was one of the best experiences I have ever had. I got to zipline, river raft, sit on hammocks that fell into the water, and swim through a waterfall. The park offered a delicious lunch, and there were so many activities for different age groups.

June 26

Climbing Ancient Mayan Ruins

On our third day in Cancún, my family and I went to visit some Mayan ruins. We first went to Coba, where we climbed a pyramid in sweltering heat. After coming back down, I genuinely thought my legs would give up on me. I don't think I have even been so tired in my life. After seeing Coba, we went to Tulum, and we saw so many ruins right by the beach. We walked around for a while, and then swam around in the water to cool off. Overall, one of the most tiring days, but also the most fun.
